Humor creeps in to Lipton's work and, fittingly, it is a creepy humor.

Yet for all of their technical finesse and spatial expansiveness, Lipton's scenes can feel reductive, a bit like editorial cartoons. Lipton draws with an all-over deliberateness that invokes the homogeneity she satirizes, and in a way, suffers a bit from it too. There is a palatable, strangely bland beauty to these horrors. The drawings mesmerize, in part, because of this friction, but some deviation from the sameness would come as a relief and ratchet up the work's already haunting power.
